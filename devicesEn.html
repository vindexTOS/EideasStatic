Automated systems

Automatisation of Detector System in Nuclear Physics Research Center in Juelich, Germany.

As a member of the JEDI collaboration, one of our team member, Otar Javakhishvili have played a key role in the development of the JEPO polarimeter. Now he shares his experience with us:

"This innovative device is specifically designed to measure and identify the energy of particles resulting from nuclear interactions between accelerated protons or deutrons and the carbon block target. Due to the complexity of this system, sophisticated control and monitoring systems are required. One crucial aspect of the JEPO polarimeter is the precise, low-noise, multi-channel power supply for SiPM sensors. These sensors are essential for accurate data collection and analysis. The power supply system incorporates dedicated software, enabling precise measurement, monitoring, and on/off control of each subunit. Additionally, the polarimeter's target system is critical, featuring both horizontal and vertical targets. To ensure precise control over these targets, a remote control computer is utilized, integrating a control system with precise position monitoring capabilities. Safety interlock features have been implemented to prevent accidental movements of both targets simultaneously, ensuring safe operation. In parallel, I have been actively involved in the development of a new carbon pellet target, which is currently under development. I have successfully created a precise trigger time measurement and image processing system tailored to this new target type. To achieve the desired results and enable seamless functioning of all the aforementioned systems, efficient system integration and automation are paramount. These systems share a common data structure and have the capability to access accelerator information. In "auto" mode, they can synchronize themselves with accelerator timing information and the data acquisition system. The collaborative efforts of our team in automating the detector system at the Nuclear Physics Research Center in Juelich, Germany, push the boundaries of scientific research and contribute significantly to the field of nuclear physics. Our dedication to precision and innovation drives us forward as we continue to make remarkable advancements in particle physics research."
